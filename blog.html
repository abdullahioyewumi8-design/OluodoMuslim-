Importance of “Pillar” of Islam

The Pillars of Islam are important because they form the core foundation of the Islamic faith, providing a structured framework for spiritual growth, community unity, and ethical conduct for Muslims worldwide. They serve as both a spiritual and practical guide, helping Muslims connect with Allah, uphold shared beliefs, demonstrate their devotion through specific acts of worship, foster a sense of global brotherhood, and instill values like compassion, self-discipline, and social justice...

Sunnah practices we should revive

Sunnah practices to revive include speaking kindly or staying silent, offering a salaam first, using a miswak for dental care, eating with the right hand and sitting to eat, sleeping on the right side, starting and ending a journey by praying, greeting guests warmly, performing wudu before prayer, and avoiding waste, especially of water. Other practices involve saying "Bismillah" before actions, not criticizing food, suppressing yawns, wearing garments starting with the right side, visiting the sick, and comforting stressed hearts.

Good character (Akhlaq) is paramount in Islam because it's a central aspect of faith, a primary reason for the Prophet Muhammad's mission, and a direct path to spiritual reward in the Hereafter.
